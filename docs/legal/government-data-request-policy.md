## Purpose

This policy defines how we handle requests from public authorities (law enforcement,
government agencies, courts, or regulators) for the personal data / personal information
of users, including any data made available to us by Meta.

## 1. Legality review

Before responding to any request, we review it for legal validity — confirming the
requesting authority's identity and jurisdiction, that proper legal process was used
(e.g. subpoena, warrant, or court order), and that the request is within scope. We do
not act on informal or unverified requests.

## 2. Challenging unlawful requests

If a request appears unlawful, overbroad, vague, or otherwise improper, we will
challenge, narrow, or refuse it through the appropriate legal channels before any
disclosure is made.

## 3. Data minimization

We disclose only the minimum personal data strictly necessary to comply with a valid
request. We do not volunteer additional information beyond what is legally required.

## 4. Documentation

We record each request and retain the record for _[e.g. 3]_ years, including:
- date received and the requesting authority,
- the legal basis / process cited,
- the specific data requested,
- our response and the legal reasoning for it, and
- the individuals involved in the decision.

## User notification

Where we are legally permitted to do so, we notify affected users of a request for
their data.

## Review

This policy is reviewed at least annually and updated as our practices or legal
obligations change.
